SafePaws: Deep Learning System for Stray Dog Behavior Monitoring

Overview Link to heading

SafePaws is a research-driven project focused on public safety and animal welfare. It utilizes CCTV feeds to analyze stray dog movements in real-time, categorizing behavior as calm or aggressive and predicting potential conflict zones using historical data.

My Role Link to heading

  • Lead researcher and developer for the end-to-end computer vision pipeline.
  • Developed a custom behavioral classification model by fine-tuning ResNet50V2 on a curated dataset of 3,200+ images.
  • Implemented BoT-SORT for real-time velocity tracking and proximity analysis between humans and animals.
  • Integrated LSTM networks to forecast high-risk “danger zones” based on spatial-temporal patterns.
  • Authored the technical research paper, including the mathematical modeling for the heuristic alert system.

Impact Link to heading

  • Created a proactive safety solution that acts as an offline AI interface for local monitoring, reducing reliance on constant cloud processing.
  • Demonstrated high accuracy in aggressive behavior detection, providing a scalable model for urban safety in smart cities.
  • Proposed a data-driven approach to animal control that prioritizes intervention over reactive measures.
